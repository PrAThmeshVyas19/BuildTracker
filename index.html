About Our Foundation

Lokmanya Cultural Foundation was born from a vision to create a vibrant platform where India's rich cultural heritage could thrive and evolve. In August 2024, we officially launched our foundation with a memorable ceremony where our logo was unveiled by the renowned actress Mrunal Kulkarni and acclaimed theater director Karan Thakur. What started as a dream to bridge the gap between traditional art forms and contemporary audiences has grown into a movement that touches lives across communities.

Our Mission

To preserve, promote, and propagate India's diverse cultural traditions through innovative programs that engage communities, nurture artistic talent, and create platforms for cultural expression. We strive to make cultural education accessible to all, fostering appreciation for our heritage while encouraging contemporary interpretations that resonate with modern audiences.

Our Vision

To be a leading cultural institution that celebrates India's artistic legacy while pioneering new pathways for cultural engagement. We envision a world where traditional arts thrive alongside contemporary expressions, where every individual has access to cultural enrichment, and where artists find sustainable platforms to showcase their talent and pass on their knowledge to future generations.

Our Legacy

We believe that culture is not just about preserving the past, but about creating meaningful connections in the present and inspiring future generations. Based in Pune, the cultural capital of Maharashtra, we draw inspiration from the city's rich legacy of arts, education, and social reform. Our foundation honors this legacy while embracing innovation and inclusivity in all our endeavors.

Featured Performances

The Poetry Rain of Bharat

A cultural exchange with Sri Lankan scholars

Date: Concluded on 21st November 2024

Venue: Samavsaran Auditorium, Pune

This event brought Sri Lankan scholars and poets to India for a tour across five cities, celebrating cultural harmony through poetry. The grand finale in Pune featured a Kathak performance and a Sitar recital.

Aatmabodhi - The Soul Awakens

Annual solo classical dance festival

Date: 29th November 2024

Venue: Pune

Curated by Smt. Abha Auti, this edition commemorated the birth centenary of Guru Rohini Bhate. It featured renowned classical solo dancers from India and abroad, celebrating the depth and devotion of solo artistry.

Maharashtra Times Essay Writing Competition

Inspiring the youth of Maharashtra

Date: 20th April 2025

This competition, organized with Maharashtra Times, aimed to inspire young minds to share their vision for a progressive future. It was open to students from age 9 up to graduation to ensure fairness and inclusivity.

Maay Marathi Kala Sangam

A three-day cultural festival

Date: 24th-26th February 2025

Venue: Times of India building

This festival celebrated Shivaji Maharaj Jayanti and Marathi Bhasha Divas, attracting over 35,000 visitors. It featured live art, weapon exhibitions, and a showcase of sculpture, music, and poetry.

Yog Yogi Festival

A Mahashivratri celebration

Date: 25th February 2025

A tribute to Mahashivratri intertwining spirituality with performing arts. The evening featured a graceful Kathak presentation 'Bhole Shiva' and an energetic Pakhwaj recital 'Shivoham' accompanied by Kathak.

Rujwaat

Gudi Padwa Celebration with 'Swarswapna'

Date: 30th March 2025

Venue: Samavsaran Auditorium, BORI

A celebration for the Marathi New Year featuring 'Swarswapna', a violin orchestra of young artists aged 8 to 18, conceived by Smt. Swapna Datar. A prize for the best ethnic attire added to the festive charm.

Akshay Gani Vasant Gani

A tribute to timeless Marathi melodies

Date: 30th April 2025

Venue: Samavsaran Auditorium, BORI

A soulful journey through evergreen songs, celebrating the musical legacy of eminent artists named Vasant: Vasant Desai, Vasant Pawar, and Vasant Prabu. The event was filled with nostalgia and the charm of legendary Marathi music.

When Dance Comes Alive

Documentary film series on dance gurus

Date: 4th & 5th July 2025

Venue: Firodia Auditorium, BORI

A two-day event screening documentaries on eminent dance gurus. Day one featured 'Kathak - Adi - Kathak' on Guru Smt. Roshan Datye, and day two showcased 'Vyomgami' on Bharatnatyam Guru Dr. Sucheta Bhide - Chapekar.

Amrutsiddhi Sohala

Celebrating author Achyut Godbole

Date: 15th August 2025

Venue: Pune

An event to celebrate the 75th birthday of renowned author Achyut Godbole. The celebration featured insightful speeches from chief guests Padma Vibhushan Dr. Anil Kakodkar and Dr. Anand Nadkarni, along with a special book release.

Sathi Kishor Pawar Pratishthan

Birth centenary of a freedom fighter

Date: 16th August 2025

An event marking the birth centenary of freedom fighter Sathi Kishor Pawar, with Hon. Sharadchandra Pawar as Chief Guest. Eminent speakers discussed the Sugar Industry and Labor Movement, complemented by soulful Kirtan and musical presentations.

Ganapati Idol Making

Workshop with actor Raqesh Bapat

Date: 24th August 2025

Venue: Karve Shikshan Hall

A unique, hands-on workshop where over 70 participants under 14 years learned to make their own eco-friendly Ganapati idols under the guidance of actor and artist Raqesh Bapat.

Kalawant Pathak Waadan

Ganesh Festival Celebration

Date: 4th September 2025

Venue: Bhandarkar Oriental Research Institute

A magnificent Ganesh Festival celebration featuring a vibrant performance by the 'Kalawant Dhol Pathak'. The event included various artists, creating an energetic atmosphere filled with music, rhythm, and festive spirit.
